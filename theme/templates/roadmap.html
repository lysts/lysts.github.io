2024 Roadmap

An overview: documentation of past work and the tentative plans toward an effective learning process for pursuing a career in software development.

I graduated in July, with a biomedical sciences degree with a specialisation in neuroscience honours. My final months were spent adjusting to life in the lab, sprint-teaching myself Python and data science skills to conduct my research and contribute to a widely used analysis pipeline.

It was rewarding, being able to apply the knowledge I had collected throughout the COVID-19 era. Intensely hands-on, I celebrated interdisciplinary collaboration and the practise of science. I was made aware of both the hardships and joys of a career dedicated to science. Unexpectedly, I was rapidly drawn to the creative process of providing programming tools for facilitating research, such as data visualisation. After speaking to informaticians, creative coders, and software engineers, I decided to put my endeavours of doing a phD aside to pursue a career in tech.

After an intense period of job hunting and familiarising myself with the industry, talking to friends, colleagues, and engineers in the software engineering field, I realised that my heart truly drew me to front-end engineering.

As of December 4th, I managed to complete about 25% of the online bootcamp described below within 1.5 weeks. Considering this progression rate, as well as time taken to practise and create portfolio projects, I envision myself completing the course by the end of January.
